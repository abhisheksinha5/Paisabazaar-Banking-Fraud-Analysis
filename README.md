# 🏦 Paisabazaar Credit Score Analysis & Classification

## 📌 Project Overview

Credit scores are one of the most important indicators used by financial institutions to evaluate an individual's creditworthiness. They directly influence loan approvals, credit limits, interest rates, and access to financial products.

This project analyzes customer credit-related data to understand how demographic, financial, and behavioral factors influence credit scores. Using Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) and data visualization techniques, the project uncovers patterns and relationships that can support better credit assessment and risk management.

---

## 💼 Business Problem

Financial institutions process applications from customers with diverse financial backgrounds. Accurately assessing creditworthiness is essential to reduce default risk and make informed lending decisions.

Factors such as annual income, credit utilization ratio, repayment behavior, delayed payments, and loan history collectively influence credit scores. Understanding these relationships through data analysis can help improve credit score classification and customer risk assessment.

---

## 🎯 Business Objective

**Improve credit score classification and strengthen credit risk assessment by identifying the key factors that influence customer creditworthiness.**

---

## 📊 Dataset Information

The dataset contains customer demographic, financial, and behavioral information, including:

- Age
- Annual Income
- Number of Credit Cards
- Credit Utilization Ratio
- Outstanding Debt
- Monthly Balance
- Delayed Payments
- Loan History
- Credit Score Category

---

## 📝 Situation

Financial institutions process thousands of customer applications and require reliable methods to evaluate creditworthiness. Traditional assumptions, such as higher income automatically leading to better credit scores, often fail to capture the complexity of customer financial behavior.

## 🎯 Task

Analyze customer credit data to identify the factors affecting credit scores and derive insights that can improve credit score classification and credit risk assessment.

## ⚙️ Actions Taken

- Performed initial data inspection and created a data dictionary.
- Identified missing values and anomalies in the dataset.
- Applied domain-driven data cleaning and preprocessing techniques.
- Conducted Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA).
- Used statistical summaries and visualizations to analyze patterns and relationships.
- Examined the impact of income, debt, credit utilization, and repayment behavior on credit scores.
- Created visualizations including histograms, boxplots, count plots, and heatmaps.
- Identified trends across different customer segments.

## 📈 Results

- Identified behavioral and financial indicators strongly associated with credit scores.
- Found that repayment behavior and credit utilization significantly impact creditworthiness.
- Demonstrated that higher income alone does not guarantee better credit health.
- Identified customer segments with higher credit risk profiles and opportunities for improved risk assessment.

---

## 💡 Business Impact

The insights generated from this analysis can help financial service providers:

- ✅ Improve credit score classification accuracy
- ✅ Enhance customer risk segmentation strategies
- ✅ Identify high-risk customers earlier in the lending process
- ✅ Deliver personalized financial recommendations
- ✅ Support data-driven lending decisions and reduce potential default risk

---

## 📊 Key Findings

- Customers with lower credit utilization ratios generally exhibit healthier credit profiles.
- Frequent delayed payments are strongly associated with poor credit scores.
- Higher outstanding debt levels tend to negatively impact creditworthiness.
- Higher income alone does not guarantee a better credit score.
- Behavioral variables often provide stronger indicators of credit health than demographic characteristics.
